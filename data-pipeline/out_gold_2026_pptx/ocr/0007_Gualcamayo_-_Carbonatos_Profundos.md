# PROJECT GEOLOGY

## Type of deposit -

### Regional Geology

The Gualcamayo deposit is located on the eastern edge of the Central Precordillera, characterized by thick sequences of marine platform limestones and dolomites, corresponding to the Los Sapitos Formation (Cambrian) and the San Juan Formation (Ordovician). To the west, it transitions to gray mudstones with organic matter of the Gualcamayo Formation (Ordovician), which is overlain by a sequence of clastic sediments of the Trapiche Group, beginning with conglomerates and a rhythmic succession of marine sandstones and mudstones of flysch facies. Outcropping in angular unconformity is a sedimentary sequence of rhythmically alternating fine sandstones and mudstones, from a fluvio-deltaic environment in glacial lakes, corresponding to the Volcán Formation (Carboniferous). This is followed by sandstones, quartzose sandstones, sabulitic and conglomeratic sandstones, arkose, and mudstones from a fluvial environment with palustrine intervals, belonging to the Panacán Formation (Carboniferous). This gradually transitions to blood-red sandstones alternating with white quartzose sandstones, interbedded with purplish-red mudstones at intervals of evaporites (mainly gypsum), from an arid and oxidizing continental environment, belonging to the Ojo de Agua Formation, assigned to the Permian.

Completing the column are the volcaniclastic deposits, andesitic lavas, and pyroclastic rocks of intermediate composition of the Cerro Morado Formation.

### Deposit Geology

The Gualcamayo deposit is located in a Cambrian-Ordovician stratigraphic sequence at the base of which are the dolomites and limestones belonging to the Los Sapitos Formation (Cambrian) followed by a sequence of limestones and dolomites of the San Juan Formation (Ordovician).

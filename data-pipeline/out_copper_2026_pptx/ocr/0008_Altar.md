# PROJECT GEOLOGY

## Regional Geology

The Altar Project is located within the southern flat-slab sub-domain of the Central Andes (28°S–33°30'S), specifically in the Cordillera Principal, which represents an inactive magmatic arc and associated thrust belt. The regional basement is dominated by the Permo-Triassic Choiyoi Group, comprising a lower calc-alkaline volcanic sequence related to subduction-related arc magmatism, overlain by peraluminous rhyolitic volcanics associated with post-orogenic extensional collapse. These volcanic sequences are intruded by coeval A-type and S-type granites and display widespread low-grade propylitic alteration.

Within the project area, the Choiyoi basement is overlain by rhyolitic ignimbrites and andesitic volcanics of the Pachón Formation. Regionally, the Altar-Rio Cenicero district is structurally bounded by two major north-south striking faults: the Pelambres Fault to the west and the Rio Teatinos Fault to the east, juxtaposing the Pachón Formation against Paleogene units and Paleozoic to Lower Mesozoic basement rocks, respectively.

## Deposit Geology

The Altar project represents a Miocene porphyry Cu-Au system, formed by multiple intermediate-composition subvolcanic porphyries emplaced between 11.9 and 8.9 Ma within volcanic rocks of the Pachón Formation. Mineralization is strongly controlled by inherited extensional structures that were later inverted, focusing magma emplacement and hydrothermal fluid flow.

The system displays typical Andean porphyry-style alteration zoning (potassic, propylitic and phyllic), with locally developed moderate supergene enrichment. Upper portions of the system are overlain and flanked by high- to intermediate-sulphidation epithermal mineralization, characterized by advanced argillic alteration and vein-style mineralization, reflecting the vertical continuity of the magmatic-hydrothermal system.
